muxi: ai-guided product planning

Role

I was the lead UX/UI designer and a product owner, with oversight from my creative director and TTT Studios' president. I had a secondary UX/UI designer on my team for support.

Tools

Figma, Miro, OpenAI, Clickup

Overview & Impact

Our objective was to evolve an existing proof-of-concept to a full-fledged product ready for beta. The product was first beta launched at the Vancouver Web Summit 2025.

Muxi was one of the only projects supported by the entire jury board for the event, out of 1,108 startups from 117 countries. The event saw 15,727 attendees, with 681 investors and 550 media members.

Beta-ready in 4 months

Beginning as an AI-partner for user research, the web platform originally catered towards UX/UI designer and design researchers. This proof-of-concept version of the product required a high degree of manual inputs from the user. Much of the work here was focused on the technical side of things, rather than focused on the user experience.

As the previous lead designer on this product departed abruptly, I was given a tight, 4 month timeline to lead the next phase of this product. However, things did not go as planned--the previously collated research for this product had little evidence to support the adoption and future pricing models. The original product idea had a very small scope of potential users, and provided very little added value beyond what other services could provide for free.

Redirecting our vision

To address these issues quickly, I lead multiple discovery sessions with the internal team, clarifying direction, features, and technical scope. We produced 15+ new user experience flows over two weeks, presenting them on a near-daily basis to keep conversations flowing. This resulted in a complete 180 of the product, focusing on a new target demographic, reprioritized features, and a change in scope and timeline.

New horizons

Our new direction consisted of a few main focuses

1. Build for budding product teams looking to validate their ideas.

2. AI should enhance the user's work, rather than regurgitate answers from the internet--meaning new features that build on the user's inputs.

3. The experience should be quick, linear, and provide an actionable outcome for the user.

With the new direction in mind, several large UX/UI changes needed to be in place to ensure the user could intuitively navigate through the interface.

Redefining navigation

The previous iteration focused on having minimal navigational control for ease of MVP implementation. This meant that the top bar had only basic breadcrumbs and "back" buttons.

Previous MVP iteration

To tackle this, I introduced navigation bars that cover both high level navigation (such as home dashboard and settings), as well as lower level navigation (within the current feature). This allows the user to have full control and understanding of where they are in the application, with an easy way to return to previously accessed pages.

New dashboard with left and top navigation bars, and a user settings menu

Visualizing linear flows

Previously, users were unsure which modules needed to be completed in what order, which lead to confusion when they accessed a module that was still locked.

To tackle this, we implemented a linear flow with CRUD (create, read, update, delete) functionality. This is visualized for the user in their project overview page. We also introduced additional flexibility by allowing the user to set project goals, which would customize their linear process.

New project overview, goal selection UI, and project export menu

In individual modules, users can also now see save and update buttons respective to their current progress, with their overall progress in this project reflected in the left side navigation bar.

Examples of modules with new navigation hierarchy and CRUD

I also conducted additional product research to expand the scope of the application's AI generated content, allowing us to provide the user with better insights and structured suggestions to improve the user's breadth of research.

A transformed module providing the user with guidance around industry information

Giving it some wings

To differentiate us from other AI products on the market, I focused my last few weeks polishing the overall brand, story, and visual identity. While our original design used a sparkle similar to other products, I wanted to personify the brand and create something more unique to our vision.

Old logo and catchphrase

I was inspired by the transformation of butterflies--like an idea forming on the ground, to a metamorphosis, to launching into the skies. I wanted our application to be seen like the metamorphosis phase, that allowed any user to incubate their brainstorming into a fully-fledged product idea.
